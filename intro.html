Numerical Methods

Numerical analysis is a rich store of methods employed to find the answer by purely arithmetical operations.
Thus, Numerical analysis aims to solve problems where analytical solutions are not available (using mathematical approach) or very hard mathematical process and it is capable of handling large systems of equations, different degrees of nonlinearities which are common in engineering practice. Numerical methods can handle any complicated physical geometries which are often impossible to solve analytically. It forms the basis in proffering solutions to rather complex engineering challenges and hence requires a structured learning curve to guide the students of engineering if such knowledge will be used to tackle evolving real life challenges.

Intelligent Tutoring System

To alleviate the challenges encountered in the classroom model, Intelligent Tutoring Systems (ITS) are developed.
The ITS are computer programs that are widely implemented so as to support and customized classes automatically without intervention from a human teacher. In addition to automatically customizing classes, Intelligent tutoring systems also guide students through a curriculum of instructional activities and monitor step-by-step progress of activities carried out by the student.
This System will not only be used to guide students that require the knowledge of numerical methods but also be used to test the knowledge acquired after they have been tutored by the system.
